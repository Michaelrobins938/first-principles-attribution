# Formal Submission Guidance for Technical Whitepapers

## Overview
This document provides comprehensive guidance for submitting the "A First-Principles Hybrid Attribution Framework" whitepaper to academic journals, conferences, or industry publications.

## Submission Categories

### Academic Journals
**Target Venues:**
- Journal of Marketing Analytics
- Marketing Science
- Operations Research
- Management Science
- Journal of Interactive Marketing

**Requirements:**
- Double-blind peer review (remove identifying information)
- Maximum 8,000-12,000 words
- 4-6 keywords
- 150-250 word abstract
- 3-5 page limit for conference proceedings
- LaTeX or Word manuscript templates

**Review Criteria:**
- Theoretical contribution
- Methodological rigor
- Practical relevance
- Reproducibility
- Statistical validity

### Industry Conferences
**Target Venues:**
- ACM SIGKDD Conference on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining
- International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML)
- Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS)
- Advertising Week
- GrowthHackers Conference

**Requirements:**
- 4-8 page extended abstract
- Demonstration of practical impact
- Novel algorithmic contributions
- Reproducible results
- Industry partnerships/case studies

### Preprint Servers
**Target Venues:**
- arXiv (cs.LG, stat.ML)
- SSRN (Marketing Science)
- ResearchGate
- Google AI Publications

**Requirements:**
- Open access
- No formal peer review
- Rapid dissemination
- Supplementary materials encouraged

## Formatting Requirements

### General Formatting
- **Font:** Times New Roman 12pt or Computer Modern 11pt
- **Line spacing:** Double-spaced (2.0) for body text
- **Margins:** 1 inch on all sides
- **Page numbering:** Bottom center/right corner
- **Header:** Running head with shortened title

### Title Page Requirements
- **Title:** Concise, descriptive, under 15 words
- **Authors:** Full names, affiliations, ORCID IDs
- **Corresponding author:** Complete contact information
- **Footnotes:** Funding sources, conflicts of interest
- **Keywords:** 4-6 terms from standardized vocabularies
- **JEL Classification Codes:** For economics/business papers

### Abstract Structure
- **Length:** 150-250 words
- **Structure:**
  1. Problem statement (2-3 sentences)
  2. Methodology approach (3-4 sentences)
  3. Key findings/results (2-3 sentences)
  4. Implications/contributions (1-2 sentences)

### Reference Format
- **Style:** APA, Chicago, or venue-specific format
- **Minimum:** 30-50 relevant citations
- **Recency:** 60% within last 5 years
- **Balance:** Mix of foundational and cutting-edge work
- **Self-citation:** Limit to 10-15% of total references

## Content Requirements

### Theoretical Contributions
- **Novelty:** Clearly articulate what is new
- **Rigor:** Mathematical proofs where applicable
- **Generalizability:** Broader applicability beyond specific case
- **Foundation:** Connection to established theory

### Empirical Validation
- **Dataset:** Publicly available or synthetic data
- **Baseline comparisons:** Multiple state-of-the-art methods
- **Statistical significance:** Proper hypothesis testing
- **Reproducibility:** Code and data availability statement
- **Robustness:** Sensitivity analysis and ablation studies

### Practical Impact
- **Real-world application:** Industry partnerships or case studies
- **Scalability:** Performance on large datasets
- **Usability:** Implementation complexity and deployment
- **Business value:** ROI, efficiency gains, or cost savings

## Review Process

### Peer Review Timeline
- **Submission to first decision:** 2-4 months
- **Revision cycle:** 1-2 months typically
- **Final acceptance to publication:** 1-3 months
- **Conference deadlines:** Usually 6-8 months in advance

### Common Review Criteria
- **Significance:** Importance of the problem and contribution
- **Originality:** Novel ideas and approaches
- **Correctness:** Sound methodology and analysis
- **Clarity:** Clear writing and presentation
- **Completeness:** Thorough evaluation and discussion

### Response to Reviews
- **Timeliness:** Submit within specified timeframe
- **Professionalism:** Respectful tone in all responses
- **Completeness:** Address all reviewer concerns
- **Documentation:** Point-by-point response format
- **Changes:** Highlight modifications in revised manuscript

## Ethical Considerations

### Authorship
- **Criteria:** Substantial contributions to conception, design, execution, or interpretation
- **Responsibilities:** Accountability for all aspects of work
- **Order:** Reflects relative contributions (justify if not alphabetical)
- **Verification:** All authors approve final version

### Conflicts of Interest
- **Financial:** Funding sources, employment, stock ownership
- **Personal:** Relationships with other authors or reviewers
- **Professional:** Competing interests in research area
- **Disclosure:** Transparent reporting in submission

### Data and Code Sharing
- **Availability:** Commitment to share upon reasonable request
- **Anonymization:** Protect participant privacy
- **Reproducibility:** Sufficient detail for replication
- **Licensing:** Appropriate open-source licenses

## Best Practices

### Writing Tips
- **Active voice:** More engaging and direct
- **Clear transitions:** Between sections and paragraphs
- **Precise terminology:** Consistent and accurate
- **Logical flow:** Ideas build upon each other
- **Concise expression:** Eliminate unnecessary words

### Figure and Table Guidelines
- **High resolution:** Minimum 300 DPI for images
- **Legible fonts:** Minimum 8pt, readable when shrunk
- **Informative captions:** Self-explanatory descriptions
- **Consistent style:** Uniform formatting throughout
- **Appropriate types:** Match data to visualization type

### Response Strategies
- **Gratitude:** Thank reviewers for their time
- **Transparency:** Acknowledge limitations honestly
- **Specificity:** Provide detailed responses to concerns
- **Evidence:** Support claims with data when possible
- **Collaboration:** Frame as joint effort to improve work

## Special Considerations for Attribution Research

### Privacy and Ethics
- **Data protection:** Ensure compliance with GDPR, CCPA
- **Anonymization:** Remove or encrypt personal identifiers
- **Consent:** Verify appropriate data usage permissions
- **Bias mitigation:** Address potential algorithmic bias

### Industry Collaboration
- **Partnership disclosure:** Acknowledge industry collaborators
- **Independence:** Maintain research objectivity
- **Confidentiality:** Protect proprietary information
- **Impact assessment:** Consider broader implications

This guidance document should be customized based on the specific venue and requirements for your submission.
